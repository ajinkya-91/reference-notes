What is Regex ?

A regular expression is a special text string for describing a search pattern. You can think of regular expressions as wildcards on steroids. Regular expressions are extremely useful in extracting information from text such as code, log files, spreadsheets, or even documents.

All Regex start with '/' and ends with '/'

Here are few examples

        
            /Hello/
            /\|[^\|=]*=/
            /^(?=.*\d)(?=.*[a-z])(?=.*[A-Z])(?=.[\W]).{8,}$/
            /I know it looks alien/

Don't shy away now. We will have alot more complex Regex in future post. So sit tight . . .

Our first assignment is to find how many "dolor" and "dolore" are there in below paragraph

To find any set of letters or number we just put in the letters or number in "//"

        
            /123-456/ ## Find's "123-456" in a given string.
            /Robin96/ ## Find's "Robin96" in a given string.
            /Hat/     ## Find's "Hat" in a given string.

Important point to note here is this regex "/Hat/" finds "Hat" and not "hat", meaning it is case sensitive.

So to find "dolor" regex is /dolor/

And to find "dolore" regex is /dolore/
